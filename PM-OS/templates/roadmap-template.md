# Product Roadmap Template

Your strategic communication tool for aligning teams and stakeholders.

## Why Roadmaps Matter

"The format matters as much as the content. A poorly communicated roadmap leads to misaligned teams, confused stakeholders, and missed opportunities." - Aakash Gupta

**Choose format based on audience:**
- Executive: High-level themes, business outcomes
- Engineering: Timeline, dependencies
- Sales/Marketing: Features, launch dates
- Design: User value, experience improvements

## Quick Reference by Audience

### For Executives
**Show:** Themes, business outcomes, OKR alignment  
**Hide:** Technical details, specific dates  
**Format:** Now/Next/Later or Goal-Oriented

### For Engineering
**Show:** Features, dependencies, timeline  
**Hide:** Marketing fluff  
**Format:** Timeline with dependencies

### For Sales/Marketing
**Show:** Customer-facing features, launch dates  
**Hide:** Technical debt, infrastructure  
**Format:** Timeline with feature descriptions

### For Customers
**Show:** Value delivery, problem solving  
**Hide:** Internal initiatives, specifics  
**Format:** High-level themes only

## Roadmap Best Practices

### Do:
‚úÖ Lead with "why" not just "what"  
‚úÖ Show trade-offs made  
‚úÖ Update regularly (monthly minimum)  
‚úÖ Get stakeholder buy-in  
‚úÖ Make it visual and scannable  
‚úÖ Link to OKRs/strategy

### Don't:
‚ùå Promise specific dates far out  
‚ùå Include every small feature  
‚ùå Make it static (keep it living)  
‚ùå Use jargon for external audiences  
‚ùå Ignore dependencies  
‚ùå Forget to explain the "why"

## Maintenance Schedule

**Weekly:** Review progress, update status  
**Monthly:** Update "Now" section, refine "Next"  
**Quarterly:** Major review, update all sections  
**As needed:** Adjust for strategic changes

## Tools & Formats

**Best tools by use case:**
- Quick visual: Miro, FigJam
- Integrated with work: Jira, Asana, Monday.com
- Executive presentations: ProductPlan, Roadmunk, PowerPoint
- Collaborative planning: Confluence, Notion
- Lightweight: Google Sheets, Airtable

**Format recommendations:**
- Now/Next/Later: Best for outcome-focused teams
- Timeline: Best for date-driven organizations
- Goal-Oriented: Best for OKR-driven teams
- Swimlane: Best for multiple product lines
